Vienna Published the Week's Most-Cited Paper on Scientific Truth. Twice.

Two companion papers in Nature — both from Vienna, both published the same morning — mounted the most ambitious audit of social science ever attempted.

01 — Overview

360 papers. One week.

Vienna science rarely makes this kind of noise in a single week. Between March 30 and April 6, databases indexed 360 papers from Vienna-based researchers. That's roughly a third more than the weekly baseline of around 270. Some of that is catch-up indexing. But not all of it.

02 — Institution

A statistical dead heat

In normal weeks, one institution dominates. This week, two tied. Uni Wien and MedUni Wien both produced exactly 137 indexed papers — a result so unusual that analysts flagged it immediately. It likely reflects the shared authorship on the two major Nature papers, which list researchers from both institutions.

03 — Funding

TU Wien wins big in Brussels

TU Wien produced 53 papers this week — but the real news from Mariahilfer Straße is the funding. Two new European grants landed in the pipeline: an ERC Advanced Grant worth several years of support for superconductor modeling, and an MSCA Postdoctoral Fellowship for quantum computing. Brussels, it seems, has been paying attention.

04 — Long tail

The long tail of Vienna research

Beyond the top three: BOKU (18 papers), OeAW (16), WU Wien (11), IST Austria (6). IST's number looks small but represents high-impact output from a campus of 700 scientists. A physics/ML paper on calorimeter simulation from OeAW drew attention outside the field.

Featured Research

The Papers That Defined the Week

Nature · Social Science

Investigating the analytical robustness of the social and behavioural sciences

Balazs Aczel, Barnabas Szaszi, Felix Holzmeister et al. — Uni Wien, MedUni Wien, WU Wien
April 1, 2026

5 citations in 5 days. The study tested whether the same data, analyzed by different teams using different methods, produces different conclusions. It does — and the implications reach into every empirical field.

doi.org/10.1038/s41586-025-09844-9
Nature · Replication

Investigating the replicability of the social and behavioural sciences

Large multi-lab consortium incl. Uni Wien
April 1, 2026

The companion paper asked the harder question: not whether the analysis holds, but whether the finding itself can be reproduced from scratch by a different lab. The answer, as with so much in social science, is: sometimes.

doi.org/10.1038/s41586-025-10078-y

ICT25 projects underway

No new projects were added to the WWTF portfolio this week. The current cohort spans ICT, life sciences, and environmental research. The most recently formalized project — ICT25-081, a cryptography project led by Azari — formally begins October 1.

FWF Is Quiet. Vienna Is Not.

The domestic grant pipeline has been silent since March 23, with the next FWF batch expected sometime in April. But this week showed what Vienna's research institutions can do in the meanwhile — and what happens when large-scale, international collaboration is rooted in a single city.
